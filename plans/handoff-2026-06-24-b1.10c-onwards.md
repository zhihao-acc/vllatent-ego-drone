## 4. Required First Investigation

Do this before changing code or launching new training:

1. **Reproduce the run summary locally from artifacts.**
   - Parse `runs/h20_b1_latent_lr2e-4_nan_20260630/val_metrics.jsonl`.
   - Locate the first NaN in `train.log` / `train_metrics.jsonl`.
   - Confirm `ckpt_best.pt` is loadable and from epoch 16.

2. **Audit validation split quality.**
   - Print source counts for train/val.
   - Run per-source persistence and model cosine on val.
   - Identify whether one or two sources dominate the negative margin.
   - Check whether `ski03` is stale, mismatched, or unusually easy/hard.

3. **Audit the metric and baseline.**
   - Verify `evaluate()` compares predicted `z_hat_{t+k}` to GT `z_{t+k}` and persistence
     `z_t` to GT `z_{t+k}` with identical masking/weighting.
   - Check whether averaging per-frame/per-patch/per-sample can bias the reported margin.
   - Consider whether raw DINOv3 latents are so frame-persistent that absolute prediction must be
     residualized or evaluated with an additional motion-sensitive diagnostic. Do not change DoD
     without updating the plan.

4. **Audit train/val behavior.**
   - Evaluate `ckpt_best.pt` on train and val. If train also fails persistence, the model/objective
     is underfitting or mis-specified. If train beats persistence and val does not, it is a data
     scale/split/generalization issue.
   - Compare `--no-action-film` against action-FiLM on a small controlled run. MegaSaM deltas are a
     soft conditioning hint in B-1; noisy action conditioning may hurt.

5. **Audit numerics.**
   - Re-run a short controlled smoke with `--amp-dtype fp32` or lower LR to see if NaN disappears.
   - Check gradients/activations around the first NaN.
   - Consider making the trainer fail fast on non-finite loss before corrupting later epochs.

6. **Research before redesign.**
   - Review relevant local reports and, if needed, current literature/docs on latent video
     prediction metrics, persistence baselines, JEPA-style latent predictors, residual prediction,
     and action-conditioned world models.
   - Bring the diagnosis back to the user before replanning.

## 5. Candidate Hypotheses - Do Not Treat As Decisions Yet

These are prompts for investigation, not approved changes:

- **Persistence is too strong at 5 Hz / 4-step horizon.** The model may learn useful motion but
  raw DINOv3 latent cosine rewards "copy current frame" too much.
- **The 57M predictor is too large for ~22K windows.** The plan already says B-1 is
  architecture-validation and data-scale, not a deployable convergence regime.
- **The objective may need residual or delta prediction.** Predicting `z_{t+k} - z_t`, adding a
  residual path, or reporting motion-sensitive diagnostics might better test dynamics, but this
  requires plan discussion.
- **Action-FiLM may be noisy.** MegaSaM scale/drift is non-load-bearing for B-1 targets, but it
  may still degrade conditioning.
- **The validation fold may be high variance.** The plan called for rotating 2-3 folds; one fold
  with `ski03` in val may not be representative.
- **Numerics are not clean.** The late NaN under bf16/LR 2e-4/WD 0.05 needs a fix even though
  `ckpt_best` predates it.
- **Data quality or duplicates may dominate.** Source-level QC passed visually, but per-source
  latent/persistence diagnostics are still needed.
